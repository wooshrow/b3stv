[13-05-2025] Exam-1 Scope:

  • Basic concepts: test, test-set, test requirement (TR), coverage, coverage subsuming.
  • Graph-based testing. A&O; 1st-Ed Ch 1, 2.1 – 2.3 (2nd-Ed: Ch 2,3,5,7.1 – 7.3).
  • Partition-based testing. A&O; 1st-Ed Ch 4 (2nd-Ed Ch 6).
  • Finite State Machine (FSM) and Extended FSM for model-based testing.
  • Dataflow-based testing. A&O; 1st-Ed Ch 2.2.2, 2.4.2, 7.1 (2nd-Ed: 7.2.3, 7.4.2. There is no matching chapter in 2nd-Ed for 1st-Ed's 7.1, so you need to get a copy for that yourself, one is in the files-section). This includes integration testing and testing OO programs as discussed in the slides in week-3.
  • Generating complex inputs (regular expression and context free grammar based), as in the slides, including concepts of grammar-based test coverage.
  • Formalization of program specifications (pre- and post-conditions).

Excluded: MacCabe/Cyclomatic complexity.

[07-05-2025] As promised, several links to research literature, below. Just to be clear, this is not exam material. Just for your curiosity.

If you are interested, a standard starting literature for automated search-based test (SBT) generation is the survey paper by McMinn. It is a bit old by now, but it is still a good starting point. Many of the more modern work on automated testing uses this idea of search-based.

McMinn, Search‐based software test data generation: a survey. In the Journal of Software testing, Verification and reliability, 14(2), 2004.

Complementary to SBT is the symbolic approach, closely related to the verification approach from your 2nd part of the course. It is also an automatic approach, though each approach (SBT vs symbolic) has its own strength and weakness.

Baldoni et al. A survey of symbolic execution techniques. ACM Computing Surveys (CSUR), 51(3), 2018.
